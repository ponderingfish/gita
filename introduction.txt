Invocation

लक्षमीनारायणं नत्वा पूर्णबोधान्गुरुनपि ।
कुर्मः श्रीकृष्णगीतायाभाष्याद्युक्तार्थसंग्रहम् ॥

lakṣamīnārāyaṇaṃ natvā pūrṇabodhāngurunapi |
kurmaḥ śrīkṛṣṇagītāyābhāṣyādyuktārthasaṃgraham ||

Word-by-word: लक्षमीनारायणं नत्वा - Bowing to Laxmi Narayana; पूर्णबोधान् + गुरुन् + अपि - also to guru Madhwacharya (poorNa = full; bodha = knowledge); कुर्मः - we will do;  श्रीकृष्णगीताया - Shri Krishna's Gita; भाष्या + आदि + उक्त + अर्थ = speak the meaning given in the bhAshya (of Madhwacharya) and other; संग्रहम् - in brief. 

Translation: Bowing down to Laxmi Narayana and also to guru Madhwacharya, we will give the meaning of Shri Krishna's Bhagavad Gita briefly as explained in the Bhaashya (of Madhwacharya) and others. 




Introduction

"स्त्रीशूद्रब्रह्मबंधूनां त्रयी न श्रुतिगोचरा । 
कर्मश्रेयसि मूढानां श्रॆय ऎवं भवॆदिह ।
इति भारतमाख्यानं क्रपया मुनिना कृतम् ॥" 
[भा. पु. १. ४. २५]

इति भागवतोक्तया, ‘ वेदार्थज्ञानानधिकारणां स्त्रीशूद्रादीनां धर्मज्ञानद्वारा मोक्षॊ भवेद् ’ इति भगवान्वेदव्यासः संभवसभाऽरण्यविराटॊद्यॊगभीष्म द्रोणकर्णशल्यगदासौषुप्तिकशांति अनुशासनिकाश्वमेधिकमौसलमहाप्रस्थानस्वर्गारोहणभविष्यत्पर्वभेदेनाष्टादशपर्वत्मकं भरतं तत्र भीष्मपर्वणि भारतपारिजतमधुभूतां सर्वभारतार्थसंग्रहभूतां श्रीकृष्णार्जुनसंवादरूपां भगवद्गीतां निबबन्ध । ‘ तत्र पूर्वषट्के ज्ञानॊपायॊक्तिः । द्वितीयष्ट्के साधनोक्तिः । तृतीयषट्के प्रागुक्तस्य प्रपञ्चनं क्रियत ’ इति विवेकः । तत्राद्याध्यायो द्वितीये कतिपयश्लोकाश्च कृष्णेनार्जुनबोधने प्रसक्तिसम्पादनाय प्रवृत्ताः । तत्रायमर्थः ।


"strīśūdrabrahmabaṃdhūnāṃ trayī na śrutigocarā |
karmaśreyasi mūḍhānāṃ śreya evaṃ bhavediha |
iti bhāratamākhyānaṃ krapayā muninā kṛtam ||" 
[bhā. pu. 1. 4. 25]

iti bhāgavatoktayā, ‘ vedārthajñānānadhikāraṇāṃ strīśūdrādīnāṃ dharmajñānadvārā mokṣo bhaved ’ iti bhagavānvedavyāsaḥ saṃbhavasabhā:'raṇyavirāṭodyogabhīṣma droṇakarṇaśalyagadāsauṣuptikaśāṃti anuśāsanikāśvamedhikamausalamahāprasthānasvargārohaṇabhaviṣyatparvabhedenāṣṭādaśaparvatmakaṃ bharataṃ tatra bhīṣmaparvaṇi bhāratapārijatamadhubhūtāṃ sarvabhāratārthasaṃgrahabhūtāṃ śrīkṛṣṇārjunasaṃvādarūpāṃ bhagavadgītāṃ nibabandha | ‘ tatra pūrvaṣaṭke jñānopāyoktiḥ | dvitīyaṣṭke sādhanoktiḥ | tṛtīyaṣaṭke prāguktasya prapañcanaṃ kriyata ’ iti vivekaḥ | tatrādyādhyāyo dvitīye katipayaślokāśca kṛṣṇenārjunabodhane prasaktisampādanāya pravṛttāḥ | tatrāyamarthaḥ |

Word-by-word: स्त्री + शूद्र + ब्रह्मबंधूनां - women, shudras, men belonging to brahmaNa, kshatriya, vaishya classes without having undergone the proper samskArAs; त्रयी - these three; न - not; श्रुति - vedas; गोचरा - attainable; कर्मश्रेयसि - for excellence in their work; मूढानां - less intelligent people; श्रॆय - benefit; ऎवं - thus; भवॆत् - let it be; इह - this; इति - thus; भारतम् + आख्यानं - the Mahabharata was told; क्रपया - out of compassion; मुनिना - by sage (Vedavyasa); कृतम् - done.

इति भागवतोक्तया - the Bhagavata says this; वेदार्थ + ज्ञान - the meaning and special knowledge in the Vedas; अनधिकारणां - do not have the authority; स्त्री + शूद्र + आदीनां - women, shudras, and others; धर्म - dharma; ज्ञान - jnana;  द्वारा - through; मोक्षॊ - liberation; भवेद् - exists; इति - this; भगवान्वेदव्यासः - Lord Vedavyasa; संभव + सभा आरण्य + विराट + उद्यॊग + भीष्म द्रोण + कर्ण + शल्य + गदासौषुप्तिक + शांति अनुशासनिक + अश्वमेधिक + मौसल + महाप्रस्थान + स्वर्गारोहण + भविष्यत्पर्व + भेदेन + अष्टादश + पर्वत्मकं भरतं - adi, sabha, vana, viraata, udyoga, bheeshma, droNa, karNa, shalya, sauptika, stri, shaanti, anushasana, ashvamedhika, ashramavasika, mausala, mahaprasthanika - the eighteen parvas of the Mahabharata;  तत्र - there; भीष्मपर्वणि - in the Bheeshma parva; भारत + पारिजत + मधु + भूतां -   like the nectar of the paarijata flower; सर्व + भारत + अर्थ + संग्रह + भूतां - is the essence of the entire Mahabharatha; श्रीकृष्ण + अर्जुन + संवाद + रूपां - in the form of a dialogue between Shri Krishna and Arjuna; भगवद्गीतां निबबन्ध - he put forward the Gita; तत्र पूर्वषट्के - there in the first six chapters; ज्ञान + उपाय + उक्ति: - method to gain knowledge is explained; द्वितीयष्ट्के साधनोक्तिः - in the second six chapters, the method for practising (sadhana); तृतीयषट्के - in the third set of six chapters;  प्रागुक्तस्य - of the previous utterance; प्रपञ्चनं - explanation; क्रियत - is done; इति + विवेकः - this is the distinction; तत्र + आद्य + द्वितीये + अध्यायो - there, in the first and second chapters; कतिपय - few; श्लोकाश्च - shlokas and; कृष्णेन - by Krishna; अर्जुन -  Arjuna; बोधने - instruction; प्रसक्ति + सम्पादनाय प्रवृत्ताः - exist to establish the topic of conversation; तत्र + अयम् + अर्थः - this is the meaning. 


Translation: Women, shudras, and men belonging to the brahmaNa, kshatriya, vaishya classes who have not undergone the proper samskArAs - these three classes are not eligible for studying the Vedas. Out of compassion, sage Vedavyasa composed the Mahabharata, by which the unintelligent people can attain excellence in their work. [Bhagavata Purana 1.4.25].

This is said in the Bhagavata PuraNa. The path to liberation (mokSha) for women, shudras, and others (brahma bandhus) who do not have the authority to study the Vedas is through the knowledge of dharma. In the eighteen parvAs of the Mahabharata, the Bhagavad Gita is in the Bheeshma parva in the form of a dialogue between Shri Krishna and Arjuna and is like the nectar of the pArijata flower (a celestial flower). There, in the first six chapters, the method of gaining knowledge (jnana) is explained. In the second set of six chapters, the method of diligent practice (sAdhana) is explained. And, in the third set of six chapters, all that was previously explained is further elaborated. This is the distinction. And, there, a few shlokas of the first and second chapters establish the topic of conversation i.e., the instructions given by Krishna to Arjuna. This is the meaning.
